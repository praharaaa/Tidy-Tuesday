# TidyTuesday: Average Honey Prices in the US

## Overview
This visualization tracks the average retail price of honey per pound across US states from 1998 to 2012.

## Key Findings
- **Price More Than Doubled**: The average honey price rose from approximately **$0.83 per pound in 1998** to **$2.37 per pound in 2012**, representing a significant increase in just 14 years.
- **Growing Price Disparity**: The gap between the cheapest and most expensive states widened considerably over the period, suggesting increasing regional variation in honey pricing.
- **Consistent Upward Trend**: Despite minor fluctuations (notably around 2004-2005), the overall trajectory shows a steady climb in honey prices throughout the 14-year period.

## Data Source
- Honey Production dataset from [TidyTuesday 2018-05-21](https://github.com/rfordatascience/tidytuesday/tree/main/data/2018/2018-05-21).
- Accessed in R with the `tidytuesdayR` package.

## Tools
- R (tidyverse, ggplot2, scales, ggtext, showtext)
- ggplot2 for visualization
- dplyr for data wrangling and aggregation
